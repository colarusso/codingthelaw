Great! After the lead inspection is complete you have a right to know the results. You may have to move out during deleading. Your landlord must pay reasonable moving expenses, anywhere you go. Make sure your request is in writing. If a child under 6 years old lives in the home and the landlord does not schedule a licensed lead inspector to inspect the tenant may either:

1. Ask your local Board of Health or the Childhood Lead Poisoning Prevention Program for a free lead inspection.

2. Do the testing themselves with a home lead test kit. These can be purchased online or at a hardware store.

3. Hire a licensed lead inspector. If the inspector finds lead, the landlord must pay you back for the inspection.

The landlord must remove or cover any dangerous levels of lead that are found. If they refuse to do this, a tenant should contact a local attorney.

Do you feel as though your landlord has retaliated against you for reporting a violation of the law?
See Massachusetts General Law 186 s. 18 Massachusetts General Law 186 s. 18. Reprisals Against Tenants for Reporting violations. MGL 186 s. 18 prohibits threats and acts of reprisal against a tenant who reports a violation of law or commences a judicial or administrative proceeding against the property owner. The landlord may not terminate tenancy, increase rent, or substantially alter the terms of the lease within a 6 month time frame, otherwise a presumption is created that the property owner must rebut with clear and convincing evidence there was no reprisal.
